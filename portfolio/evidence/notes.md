# Interview Notes — Week 6

## Participant 1

**Date**: [03/12/2025]
**Context**: Prefers dark mode
**Consent**: ✅ Confirmed verbally
**Duration**: ~10 minutes

---

### Q1: Last time using task manager?

**Response**: A few weeks ago, to remind them of an event

**Observations**: Poor contrast in dark mode

**Themes**: `contrast`

---

### Q2: Biggest frustration?

**Response**: No way of sorting tasks in order of importance

**Observations**: Has to spend a long time trying to find important events/tasks as they are not automatically filtered

**Themes**: `searching`, `completion`

---

### Q3: Last time lost track of important event?

**Response**: A couple of months ago, regarding a project at work. This resulted in their progression being delayed for a period.

**Observations**: Clear frustration

**Themes**: `confirmation`

---

### Q4: Work without mouse/trackpad?

**Response**: Found it difficult to navigate apps as the ones being used did not have built in accessibility features. This resulted in having to tab past open webpages, bookmarks, etc in the header before being able to access main content

**Observations**: Although took more time to access page content, did not seem like a massive issue because they could easily track their location on the site.

**Themes**: `keyboard`, `accssibility`

---

### Q5: Use of task manager in bright sunlight?

**Response**: Found this very difficult due to lack of appropriate dark mode contrast to the extent that they had to find somewhere shaded just to view their task manager

**Observations**: Frustration, poor GUI

**Themes**: `contrast`, `visibility`

---

### Q6: Feature add-on for ease of use?

**Response**: Modified contrast in dark mode, or add a high-contrast feature.

**Observations**: This would circumvent previous issues

**Themes**: `contrast`

---

## Summary (Participant 1)

**Top pain points**:

1. No way of sorting tasks on priority
2. Poor contrast in dark mode

**Job story ideas**:

- "When dealing with imporatant events, I want a way of prioritising tasks so I do not miss anything important."

## Participant 2

**Date**: [06/12/2025]
**Context**: Poor eyesight
**Consent**: ✅ Confirmed verbally
**Duration**: ~10 minutes

---

### Q1: Last time using task manager?

**Response**: A couple of days ago, to set deadlines for work

**Observations**: Difficulty reading input when adding tasks, lack of confirmation

**Themes**: `accessibilty`, `spacing`

---

### Q2: Biggest frustration?

**Response**: Reading what tasks are due next, due to a small font size

**Observations**: Issues with reading, screenreader potential workaround

**Themes**: `font size`, `accessibility`

---

### Q3: Last time lost track of important event?

**Response**: A few weeks ago, causing a missed deadline. This was due to no confirmation that a task was added as they misclicked the add button.

**Observations**: Frustration at lack of inclusivity in design.

**Themes**: `confirmation`

---

### Q4: Work without mouse/trackpad?

**Response**: Not been an issue as far as they can remember, they imagine it wouldn't be easy especially because of eyesight issues

**Observations**: This would be difficult without any form of accessibility features

**Themes**: `accssibility`

---

### Q5: Use of task manager in bright sunlight?

**Response**: The use of a task manager in direct sunlight is not possible. This is something they have had to accept, they have to be indoors to have any chance at reading the screen

**Observations**: Frustration, they have lived with for an extended period of time.

**Themes**: `contrast`, `visibility`, `readability`

---

### Q6: Feature add-on for ease of use?

**Response**: Screenreader or customisable font sizes.

**Observations**: Either solution assists their issue with reading tasks.

**Themes**: `accessibility`, `readability`

---

## Summary (Participant 2)

**Top pain points**:

1. Difficulty using task managers due to small font size and no screenreader builtin
2. Tracking next due events

**Job story ideas**:

- "When using a task manager, I want some way of understanding what the task is for without straining my eyes so I can organise my workflow more effectively."
